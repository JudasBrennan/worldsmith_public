## Notes

- Largest moon of Neptune; largest retrograde-orbiting moon in the solar system
- Inclination 157.3° means it orbits opposite to Neptune's rotation
- Nearly circular orbit (e ≈ 0) despite likely being a captured KBO
- Captured origin means significant initial tidal heating (now dissipated)
- One of the coldest surfaces measured in the solar system (38 K)
- Density (2.06 g/cm³) → WorldSmith "Mixed rock/ice" class
- Active geysers observed by Voyager 2 (nitrogen-driven)
